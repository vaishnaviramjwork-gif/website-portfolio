04 — Business Thinking

Case studies in how I think.

Product Analysis
Why Zomato's real product isn't food delivery
Consumer tech · Bengaluru market · Platform strategy
Platform thinking User behaviour Business model
The observation

Most people use Zomato when they're hungry. But that's not actually Zomato's business. The real product is the decision making moment before the order: browse, compare, read reviews, check photos: that's where value is created, not at checkout.

What the data reveals

Zomato's engagement metrics tell a different story than its GMV. Users spend disproportionate time in discovery: browsing restaurants they don't order from, reading reviews for places they haven't visited. This is intent data. Zomato isn't just a delivery platform: it's India's largest restaurant intent database, and every session feeds a recommendation model that compounds over time.

Business takeaway

The metric a company obsesses over is its strategy made visible. Zomato counts sessions. Swiggy counts orders. Same market, different companies.

Financial Analysis
Fintech User Retention: Beyond the Sign up
Digital Banking · User Lifecycle · Predictive Churn
Retention Modelling Financial Data Behavioral Clusters
The Thesis

In Fintech, the cost of acquisition is secondary to the cost of dormancy. A user who signs up but never transacts is a net loss. The goal of retention analysis is identifying the "Activation Event" that shifts a user from curious to committed.

Analytical Approach

By clustering users based on transaction frequency and type: recurring bills versus peer to peer transfers: we can predict churn before the account goes dark. The data suggests that users who set up an automated transfer in their first week have a 4x higher LTV. This insight shifts product focus from broad marketing to specific onboarding nudges.

Business takeaway

Transaction variety is a better predictor of long term loyalty than initial deposit size. Diversify the utility, solidify the user.

Health Tech Analysis
Patient Data Flow: Optimising Engagement
Healthcare UX · Data Interoperability · Clinical Workflows
Data Privacy Health Outcomes System Integration
The Challenge

Health apps often suffer from "notification fatigue." When data flow from wearables isn't translated into actionable insights, users disengage. The challenge is turning cold physiological metrics into warm, encouraging behavioral nudges without compromising clinical accuracy.

The Data Solution

Analysing patient drop off points reveals that data entry friction is the primary killer of engagement. By integrating third party health APIs and automating the flow: rather than asking for manual logging: we increase longitudinal data consistency. The cleaner the data flow, the more robust the predictive health models become.

Health takeaway

In healthcare, friction is more than a UX issue: it is a barrier to wellness. Automating data capture is the single biggest lever for patient compliance.

Sports Analytics
Fan Engagement: Moving from Spectator to Participant
Fan Lifecycle · Gamification · Revenue Diversification
Audience Analytics Gamification Monetization
The Observation

Traditional sports broadcasting is a one way street. Modern fan engagement data shows that younger demographics crave participation: fantasy leagues, real time voting, and social integration. The data reveals that "engaged fans" spend 30% more on merchandise and secondary services than "passive viewers."

Strategy Insight

By mapping fan interactions across social media, apps, and ticket sales, teams can build a unified fan profile. This allows for hyper personalised marketing: suggesting a jersey purchase right after a specific athlete's peak performance. Real time performance metrics aren't just for coaches: they are content for the fans.

Sports takeaway

The game happens on the field, but the business grows on the screen. Data turns a 90 minute match into a 24/7 relationship.

05 — Analyst Thinking

How I see problems.

Data → Decision

A dashboard nobody reads is not a dashboard

Every dashboard I build starts with the question: what decision does this need to make easier? Not what data do we have, but what action changes because of this screen.

"The interesting part of analysis is choosing what not to say."
Requirements

Ambiguity in a spec is a budget risk, not a style

Vague requirements feel harmless in discovery. They show up as scope creep, sprint overruns, and frustrated developers. Precision at the start is the cheapest investment in any project.

"Every mid sprint change request is a requirement that wasn't written clearly enough."
Stakeholder Communication

The brief that moves a decision is not the longest one

Leadership doesn't need the full analysis in the room. They need the three things that changed because of it. Short, opinionated write ups outperform exhaustive decks every time.

"A good summary is a point of view, not a list of findings."
